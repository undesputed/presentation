# Presentation Script — AI Assistant Project Management Tool

**Presenter:** Carrie Yu  
**Audience:** Japanese stakeholders (slides are English + Japanese)  
**Deck:** `presentation.html`

This file tells you **what to say** for each part. Use **easy English** — short sentences, simple words. The **日本語** lines help you say the same idea in Japanese when you need to.

**Tip:** Many listeners understand **simple English** better than hard words. If a word feels difficult, use the **日本語** line or the **Simple** note under **Say**.

---

## How to use this file

| Section | Time | Slides (about) |
|--------|------|----------------|
| Opening | ~2–3 min | Cover, Introduction |
| **A — Pain points** | **8–10 min** | Problem → Tool diagram → Six pain cards |
| **B — The real problem** | **8–10 min** | Root cause → Three idea cards |
| **C — Users, product, value** | **up to ~30 min** | Product → Personas → Benefits → Idea → MCP → Numbers → Why it matters |
| After this | your plan | Mission, Demo, Thank you |

**Pacing**

- Speak **slowly**. Repeat the main idea in **日本語** when it is important.
- **⏱ Check** = time hint so you don’t run too long or too short.
- **【深掘り】** = extra story — only if you have time.

# Opening (~2–3 minutes)

## Slide 1 — Cover

**Say:**  
Good morning / good afternoon. Thank you for coming. My name is Carrie Yu. I am a **software engineer**. Today I will talk about one project: an **AI assistant for project management**. It helps project managers by **connecting tools they already use**.

**Simple words:** software engineer = person who builds software; AI assistant = computer helper that talks and acts for you.

**日本語（例）:**  
本日はお時間をいただきありがとうございます。Carrie Yu と申します。本日お話しするのは、**AI アシスタント型のプロジェクト管理ツール**というプロジェクトです。

---

## Slide 2 — Introduction / Agenda

**Say:**  
I like to build **tools that help teams work smarter**, not harder. Today I will tell you three things in order. **One:** the **problem** — what people feel every day on real projects. **Two:** what we think is the **real** problem — not only “many tools.” **Three:** **who** this is for, **what** we want to build, and **why** it helps.

Later in the deck you will also see **mission, vision, and a demo** — how it works step by step.

**日本語（例）:**  
今日は、**問題**、その**本質**、そして**誰のため・何を・なぜ**を順にご説明します。

**⏱ Check:** Try to finish opening by about **3 minutes** so Parts A and B stay on time.

# Part A — Pain points (target: **8–10 minutes**)

**Slides:** “The Problem” → **The Fragmented Reality** (tool picture) → **Everyday Frustrations** (six cards)

## A1 — Section break: “Every day, something breaks”

**Say:**  
Let me be honest. In project work, **every day something goes wrong**. It is not always a **bug in the program**. Often it is the **way we work**: dates change, someone is **blocked**, an important decision is only in **chat** and some people never see it. That happens often. The question is: **how much** of this do we accept as “normal work”?

**日本語（例）:**  
プロジェクトでは毎日のように何かがうまくいきません。バグだけでなく、**進め方や連携**の問題も多いです。

**⏱ ~1:00** from the start of Part A.

---

## A2 — The Fragmented Reality (tool orbit)

**Say:**  
This slide shows **one project manager** in the middle. Around them are **many tools**: email, chat, task tools, calendar, documents, whiteboards, AI tools, and more. **Each tool does one job well.** **No single tool** shows **the whole project** in one screen.

In real life, a PM must use **all of these**. They cannot use only one app. They **change tools many times every day**. The line on the slide says **dozens of times** — that is not a joke. Real teams tell us this.

**Walk the diagram (30–45 sec):**  
Point to a few tools — for example **Backlog / Jira** for tasks, **Slack / Teams** for talk, **Calendar** for time, **Docs / Miro** for design. Say in simple English: **“The PM is the person who connects all of these.”**

**【深掘り】** *(optional +1–2 min)*  
One short story: “To answer **‘Is the sprint OK?’**, someone opens the task board, then Slack for problems, then Calendar for who is away — **three places**, three logins, three times to think again.”

**日本語（例）:**  
ツールはそれぞれ良いですが、**全体を一つで見る**役割が PM に集まっています。

**⏱ ~4:00** — you should be **almost done** with this slide.

---

## A3 — Everyday Frustrations (six cards)

**Say:**  
Now we make the picture **clear and real**. These are **everyday problems** — things that happen **every day**.

Go **one card at a time** (about **1 minute per card** = ~6 minutes for six cards; you can add a little time to reach 8–10 minutes total).

### Card 1 — Too many tools, no single view

**Say:**  
**Tasks, schedules, and messages** live in **different places**. To see **everything**, someone must open **many apps**, **by hand**, **again and again**. There is **no one screen** for all of it. So the PM must **connect** everything in their head.

**日本語:**  
タスク・スケジュール・メッセージがバラバラ。**全体**を見るには、毎回手で全部開く必要があります。

### Card 2 — Coordination entirely by hand

**Say:**  
Checking status, setting meetings, sending reminders, writing sprint updates — **all by hand**. It repeats every day. When the day is busy, this work is easy to **delay**. This work is not always on the official plan, but it **takes a lot of time** on the calendar.

**日本語:**  
進捗確認、調整、連絡、レポート——**手作業**が重なり、時間を取ります。

### Card 3 — Reports take too long

**Say:**  
Weekly or monthly reports often mean: **get data** from many tools, **think** about it, **write** a clear text — **many hours**. That is time **not** used for **risk**, **people**, or **big decisions**.

**日本語:**  
レポートに**何時間も**かかると、本当に大事な判断に使える時間が減ります。

### Card 4 — Context lost across conversations

**Say:**  
Important choices hide in **long chat threads**. Priorities change in a **meeting**, but the task list is **not updated**. Later, people forget **why** something changed and **who** will do the next step. First of all, this is not only a “people” problem — it is a **split information** problem.

**日本語:**  
**文脈**がチャットや会議に散らばり、あとから追いにくいです。

### Card 5 — Problems surface too late

**Say:**  
A person can be **blocked for two days** before the PM knows. Then the **sprint** is already in trouble. The early signs **exist** — they are just in **different places**. No one can watch **all places at the same time**.

**日本語:**  
ブロッカーに**遅れて気づく**と、スプリント全体に響きます。

### Card 6 — New tool = disruption

**Say:**  
If we try to fix this by adding **one more** big system, the **team** pays the price: learning, moving data, new habits. Often the team does not want **another** place to check — not because they hate change, but because they already have **too many** places.

**日本語:**  
**新しいツール**は学習と運用の負担があります。現場はもう疲れています。

**Closing line for Part A:**  
**Say:**  
So the pain is not “we hate software.” The pain is **the cost of connecting everything** — **time**, **mental effort**, and **risk** **between** tools.

**日本語:**  
痛みの本質は「ツールが嫌い」ではなく、**ツールの間を繋ぐ負担**にあります。

**⏱ ~10:00** from the start of Part A — then go to Part B.

# Part B — The real problem (target: **8–10 minutes**)

**Slides:** “What are we really solving?” → **What is the real problem?** (three cards + one sentence at the bottom)

## B1 — Section break: “What are we really solving?”

**Say:**  
Before we talk about “the solution,” I want to **stop for a moment**. If we only say **“there are too many tools,”** we might choose the **wrong** fix — for example, **one more** big screen that nobody asked for.

So the question is: **what are we really trying to fix?** Not only what you see on the surface — the **deeper cause**.

**日本語（例）:**  
ここで一度考えます。**本当に解決すべきこと**は何か。ツールの数だけが問題なのか。

**⏱ ~1:30** from the start of Part B.

---

## B2 — Reframe (subtitle on slide)

**Say:**  
The slide says it clearly: **“Many tools” is not the whole problem.** The bigger problem is: **the PM must connect everything alone.** The company often treats the PM as the **only bridge** between systems that **do not work as one smooth flow**.

**日本語:**  
**ツールが多いこと**より深刻なのは、**PM 一人がすべてを繋ぐ**ことです。

---

## B3 — Three cards (about **2–2.5 min each**)

### Card 1 — Most of the day is not “real” PM work

**Say:**  
Many PMs spend most of the day **checking**, **updating**, **asking people**, **writing** — not **leading**: giving clear direction, helping people who are stuck, talking to important people **on purpose**. If we **never** free time from this busy work, we do not really change the job — we only add **more messages**.

**【深掘り】**  
Simple contrast: **“moving information”** vs **“leading the project.”** You can ask: **“What do we really want PMs to do?”**

**日本語:**  
多くの時間が**確認作業**に使われ、**リーダーとしての仕事**に残る時間が少なくなります。

### Card 2 — No one place to see everything

**Say:**  
To answer one easy question — **“Are we on track?”** — the PM may need **four or five tools** and must **check by hand**. That is not because people are lazy. It is how the **system** is built. Until something **brings information together**, the PM must **do that job**.

**日本語:**  
**一つの質問**に答えるだけでも、複数のツールをまたぐ必要があります。

### Card 3 — Problems are hard to notice in time

**Say:**  
News about **delay** may be in **Slack**. Task promises are in **Backlog**. Who is free is in **Calendar**. No person can watch **every channel** with full care **all day**. So bad news comes **late** — not because people don’t care, but because **attention is limited** and **information is split**.

**日本語:**  
大事な情報が**バラバラ**で、**同時に**全部は見られません。

---

## B4 — Closing line (bottom of slide)

**Say:**  
This last line is important: **the PM becomes the only person who connects all the information** — and that **takes time away from leading**. If our project helps with **only one thing**, it should **change this**: **fewer** tabs, **less** manual connecting, **more** time for thinking and leading.

**日本語:**  
PM が**唯一の接続点**になると、リーダーの時間が減ります。**それを変えたい**——それが出発点です。

**⏱ ~8–10:00** from the start of Part B — short **pause**, then continue to the solution / product part.

# Part C — Users, product, and value (target: **up to ~30 minutes**)

**Slides (in order):**  
**The Product** → **Questions they ask every day** → **What the tool does for each user** → **What we are building** → **How the AI connects (MCP)** → **What you get back** → **Why this matters**

You can show the **hub** slide (PM → AI → tools) here for **2–3 minutes** if you like. In the file order it comes **before** “The Product” section.

---

## C0 — Optional: Hub diagram (2–3 min)

**If you show the hub slide, say:**  
We are **not** trying to throw away every tool and buy one new product. We want to put a **talking AI helper** **between** the **PM** and the **tools they already use**. You **ask one time**; the helper **works with** the tools for you. Next we explain **who** this helps and **how**.

**Simple:** “One place to talk. The AI talks to the tools behind you.”

**日本語:**  
既存ツールを**捨てる**のではなく、PM とツールの間に**対話型のAI**を置くイメージです。

---

## C1 — Section break: “Who, What, and Why” (~1 min)

**Say:**  
Now we move from **the problem** to **the product**. Three simple questions: **Who** is it for? **What** are we building — and **what** are we **not** building? **Why** does it matter for the company and for people at work?

**日本語:**  
**誰のため、何を、なぜ**をはっきりします。

**⏱** If you use a 30-minute timer for Part C, you can **start** it here.

---

## C2 — Target users: “Questions they ask every day” (~8–10 min)

**Slide:** Three roles — Project Manager, Team Leader, Senior Manager.

**Say:**  
These three roles are **real**. They show **questions we hear on real projects**. Today, answering these questions can take **hours** because **data is in many places**.

### Project Manager (~3 min)

Go through **each example question** on the slide **slowly**. After each one, say one short line: **“Today that means: open Backlog, then Slack, then Calendar…”**

Read the **red line** at the bottom loudly: **“Now: opens Backlog, Slack, and Calendar one by one to find out.”**

**日本語:**  
PM は**スプリントの障害**、**週次レポート**、**会議**、**昨日の進捗**など、毎日こうした問いに答えようとします。

### Team Leader (~2.5 min)

**Say:**  
Team leaders need to see **who is too busy**, **what is not started**, and **what to remind**. Often they **change tools** and **ask people directly**. That costs **time** — and it **breaks focus** for engineers who need quiet work time.

**日本語:**  
チームリーダーは**負荷**や**状況**を知りたいが、ツールを行き来し、人に聞くしかないことがあります。

### Senior Manager (~2.5 min)

**Say:**  
Leaders want to know **how all projects are doing**, **what risks exist**, and **which team has trouble**. Today they often **wait for a report** that someone **writes by hand** for next week. By then, the information is **already old**.

**日本語:**  
シニアマネージャーは**全体の状況**を知りたいが、**手作りのレポート**を待つことが多いです。

**【深掘り】** *(optional +3–5 min)*  
Ask a simple question: **“Which question could be faster if the data were in one place?”** Or tell **one** short true story (no names).

**⏱** After this block, about **10–12 minutes** into Part C if your full Part C is ~30 minutes.

---

## C3 — Value per role: “What the tool does for each user” (~6–8 min)

**Slide:** Three columns with green checkmarks.

**Say:**  
Now we turn from **pain** to **what we want to happen**. The idea is: **ask once — get an answer in seconds**, not hours — and where possible, **the system acts**, not only **shows text**.

Go **role by role**. Do **not** read every line like a robot — **say it in your own words**:

- **PM:** morning summary, status from Backlog, meetings from chat, problems sent to Slack.  
- **Team Leader:** who is too busy, reminders from chat, sprint progress in one question, updates through the AI.  
- **Senior Manager:** Monday summary, risk warnings, quick health questions, less need for a hand-made slide deck.

**Bridge sentence:**  
**Say:**  
We are not promising **magic**. We want to **cut repeated manual work** so each role gets **answers faster** and **fewer back-and-forth steps**.

**日本語:**  
魔法ではなく、**繰り返しの手作業**を減らし、**判断に使う時間**を増やすことです。

**⏱ ~18–20 min** into Part C.

---

## C4 — Project idea: “What we are building” (~5–7 min)

**Slide:** Four idea cards.

**Say:**  
This slide answers: **“What is the product, in simple words?”**

1. **Plain language** — You **talk** to it. You do not need a new complicated screen design. Example on slide: *What is blocking the sprint and who should I talk to?*

2. **Works with tools you already use** — Backlog, Slack, Google Calendar. The **team can keep old habits**. We mainly change **how the PM works** — that helps people **accept** the change.

3. **The AI chooses the next step** — You do not need to remember **which system** has which fact. You ask **what you want to know**.

4. **Action, not only answers** — Book meetings, send Slack, update tasks — from **one** request when possible.

**【深掘り】**  
Why **“the team does not move to a new tool”** matters in Japan: **roles**, **approval flow**, **money already spent on old tools**.

**⏱ ~24–26 min** into Part C.

---

## C5 — How it works: MCP (~4–5 min)

**Slide:** You → AI Assistant → Backlog / Calendar / Slack MCP + short text.

**Say:**  
On the **tech** side, we connect with **MCP**. The long name is **Model Context Protocol**. In simple words: it is a **common rule** so an AI can **read and write** other tools **safely**, using a **small server** for each tool. The PM does not open **five browser tabs**; the assistant uses MCP to **send the request to the right tool**.

**Walk the picture** from left to right: **your question** → **assistant** → **which MCP** → **tool**.

**One line on safety:** Rules, permissions, and company policy **still matter**. MCP is **how we connect** — it is **not** a way to skip **good governance**.

**日本語:**  
MCP は、AI が外部ツールと**安全に**つなぐための**共通のやり方**です。

---

## C6 — Numbers: “What you get back” (~3–4 min)

**Slide:** About **2 hours** per report, **45 minutes** every morning, **days → minutes** for blockers.

**Say:**  
These numbers are **examples** for discussion — to show **size**, not a **promise** for every company. You must **measure** in your own place.

- **Reports:** many hours of copy-paste work → **seconds** to create from structured data.  
- **Morning:** checking many tools → **one** short briefing.  
- **Blockers:** finding problems in **days** → in **minutes** when signals are in **one** place.

**【深掘り】**  
Ask: **“What would your team measure first — time, risk, or fewer interruptions?”**

---

## C7 — Why it matters (~3–4 min)

**Slide:** Three impact cards + closing line.

**Say:**  
Finally — **why** this is not only about **saving minutes**:

- **PMs can lead** — think clearly, talk to the right people — not only **coordinate**.  
- **Problems show up earlier** — because something can watch **across tools** in a **steady** way.  
- **The team gets fewer “what is the status?” messages** — the system can **get** context **without** asking people again and again.

**End with the line on the slide:**  
When the AI handles coordination, **the PM can become the leader they were hired to be.**

**日本語:**  
AIが調整を担うとき、PM は**本来のリーダー**に近づけます。

**⏱ ~28–30 min** — then go to **Mission & Vision** and **Demo** in your deck.
